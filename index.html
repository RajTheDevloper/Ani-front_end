(about the animes)

"Great Teacher Onizuka" is a popular Japanese anime series that aired in 1999, based on the manga of the same name by Tohru Fujisawa. The show revolves around the story of Eikichi Onizuka, a former motorcycle gang member who decides to become a teacher. Onizuka is unconventional and unorthodox in his teaching methods, often using his street smarts to solve problems and connect with his students.

"Death Note" is a critically acclaimed Japanese manga series written by Tsugumi Ohba and illustrated by Takeshi Obata. It was later adapted into an anime series that first aired in 2006. The story revolves around a high school student named Light Yagami, who discovers a mysterious notebook known as the "Death Note." This notebook grants its user the power to kill anyone by simply writing their name in it, as long as they have the person's face in mind.

"Attack on Titan," known as "Shingeki no Kyojin" in Japanese, is a popular manga series created by Hajime Isayama. It was adapted into an anime series that first premiered in 2013. The story is set in a world where humanity is on the brink of extinction due to the existence of massive humanoid creatures known as Titans, who devour humans seemingly without reason.
